# Success Metrics Defaults by Launch Tier

Default targets for launch success metrics, organized by tier and time horizon.

**Use these as starting points.** Adjust based on:
- Your company's stage (early-stage vs. enterprise scale)
- Product type (technical vs. business user)
- Market maturity (greenfield vs. competitive)
- Past launch performance (brain Section 7)

---

## Tier 1: Major Launch

### T+30 Targets (Leading Indicators)

| Metric | Default Target | Owner | How Measured |
|--------|----------------|-------|--------------|
| **Activation rate** | 40% of target segment activates feature/product | Product | Product analytics (Amplitude, Mixpanel, etc.) |
| **Feature page visits** | 10,000+ unique visits | Marketing | Website analytics (GA4) |
| **Blog post engagement** | 5,000+ reads, 300+ shares | Content Marketing | CMS analytics + social tracking |
| **Email open rate** | 25%+ (segmented audience) | Marketing | Email platform (HubSpot, Marketo) |
| **Email click rate** | 8%+ | Marketing | Email platform |
| **Sales team activation** | 80%+ of reps trained and using materials | Sales Enablement | LMS completion + manager check-ins |
| **Demo requests** | 100+ qualified demo requests | Demand Gen | Form submissions + CRM |
| **Press/media mentions** | 5-10 tier-1 publications | PR/Comms | Media monitoring tools |
| **Social engagement** | 500+ engagements (likes, shares, comments) | Social Media Manager | Social analytics |
| **Support ticket volume** | <5% of activations result in tickets | Support | Support platform (Zendesk, Intercom) |

| Metric | Default Target | Owner | How Measured |
|--------|----------------|-------|--------------|

| Metric | Default Target | Owner | How Measured |
|--------|----------------|-------|--------------|

## Metric Selection Guidelines

### Choose Metrics That Match Launch Goals

**If goal is adoption:**
- Track: Activation rate, weekly active users, feature engagement
- Don't track: Revenue (too indirect)

**If goal is revenue:**
- Track: ARR impact, expansion rate, win rate improvement
- Don't track: Social engagement (vanity metric here)

**If goal is competitive positioning:**
- Track: Win rate vs. specific competitor, feature parity score, customer switch rate
- Don't track: Overall adoption (you're targeting switchers, not full base)

**If goal is retention:**
- Track: Churn rate, CSAT, support ticket volume, time-to-value
- Don't track: New customer acquisition metrics

## Adjusting Targets by Context

### Company Stage

**Early-Stage (Pre-PMF or just post-PMF):**
- Lower adoption targets (20% → 15%)
- Higher tolerance for support volume (learn from tickets)
- Focus on qualitative feedback over quantitative metrics

**Growth Stage:**
- Standard targets (use defaults above)
- Balance adoption + revenue
- Optimize for repeatability

**Enterprise/Scale:**
- Higher adoption targets (25% → 35%)
- Stricter NPS/CSAT requirements
- Revenue impact must be measurable and significant

---

### Product Type

**Technical/Developer Product:**
- Adoption slower (20% T+30 → 10-15%)
- Engagement higher once adopted (70%+ weekly usage)
- Time-to-value longer (30 days → 60 days)

**Business User Product:**
- Adoption faster (standard targets)
- Engagement moderate (50-60% weekly)
- Time-to-value shorter (14-30 days)

**End-User/Consumer Product:**
- Adoption fastest (30%+ T+30)
- Engagement varies widely by feature type
- Time-to-value immediate (1-7 days)

---

### Market Maturity

**Greenfield (New Category):**
- Lower adoption targets (education takes time)
- Focus on usage depth over breadth
- Long sales cycles → revenue impact at T+180, not T+90

**Competitive (Established Category):**
- Standard targets
- Focus on switchers and win rate
- Faster revenue impact (existing budget)

**Commoditized:**
- Higher adoption targets (feature parity expected)
- Focus on retention and efficiency
- Revenue impact via churn prevention, not new sales

## Self-Learning: Adjusting Targets Over Time

After each launch, compare actual vs. target in brain Section 7:

```markdown
### Launch: SSO Integration | Tier: T2 | Date: 2026-05-15

**Target Metrics:**
- Adoption: 25% in 90 days
- Expansion ARR: $400K
- Feature engagement: 60% weekly

**Actual Results:**
- Adoption: 31% in 90 days (124% of target ✓)
- Expansion ARR: $320K (80% of target ✗)
- Feature engagement: 58% weekly (97% of target ~)

**Lesson:**
For enterprise technical features:
- Adoption target was achievable (even conservative)
- ARR target was too aggressive given market penetration
- Next T2 enterprise launch: reduce ARR target to $250K-$350K
```

Next launch → skill reads Section 7, adjusts targets:
```
Based on your SSO launch (T2, May 2026), I've adjusted targets:
- Adoption: 25% → 28% (your enterprise features over-index)
- Expansion ARR: $400K → $300K (more realistic for this segment)
```

## Red Flags (When to Adjust Mid-Flight)

If at T+7 or T+14 you see:

**🚨 Adoption <50% of target:**
- Action: Increase in-product promotion, send follow-up email, run emergency webinar

**🚨 Support tickets >10% of activations:**
- Action: Pause promotion, fix UX or docs, re-launch communications

**🚨 Email open rate <10%:**
- Action: Subject line failed. A/B test new subject, resend to non-opens

**🚨 Sales team not using materials:**
- Action: Enablement didn't land. Run live training, simplify materials

**🚨 Negative social sentiment:**
- Action: Identify root cause (bug? messaging? pricing?), address publicly
