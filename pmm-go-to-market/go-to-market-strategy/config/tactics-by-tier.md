# Marketing Tactics by Launch Tier

Channel playbooks organized by tier — what's required vs. optional at each level.

**Source:** Synthesized from Product Launch Playbook + your Notion GTM Process Playbook + Intercom launch tiering.

---

## Tier 1: Major Launch (Dedicated Campaign)

**Required Channels:**
- [ ] Press release + media outreach
- [ ] Blog post (thought leadership, 1000+ words)
- [ ] Customer email (segmented by persona, 2-3 variants)
- [ ] Sales enablement (live training session + deck + battlecard + demo script)
- [ ] In-product announcement (modal with multi-step guide)
- [ ] Help center + documentation (comprehensive)
- [ ] Social media campaign (LinkedIn + X, 4-6 weeks)
- [ ] Webinar or live demo event
- [ ] Landing page or website section
- [ ] Video product walkthrough (2-3 min)
- [ ] Internal all-hands announcement + FAQ
- [ ] Customer Success briefing + playbook
- [ ] Support training + FAQ
- [ ] Changelog (detailed entry)

**Optional/Conditional:**
- [ ] Paid advertising (Google, LinkedIn, if budget allows)
- [ ] Analyst briefing (if enterprise/B2B focus)
- [ ] Partner/channel communications (if partner ecosystem exists)
- [ ] Customer advisory board preview (if CAB exists)
- [ ] Event sponsorship or booth presence
- [ ] Podcast appearances or interviews
- [ ] Case study or customer testimonial video
- [ ] Executive blog post or LinkedIn article

**Timeline:** 12 weeks

**Budget:** $50K-$150K

---

## Tier 2: Moderate Launch (Integrated Campaign)

**Required Channels:**
- [ ] Blog post (product announcement, 600-800 words)
- [ ] Customer email (targeted segment, not full base)
- [ ] In-product announcement (modal or banner with CTA)
- [ ] Sales enablement (async training video + deck + talk tracks)
- [ ] Help center + documentation update
- [ ] Social posts (LinkedIn + X, organic, 2-3 posts)
- [ ] Changelog (standard entry)
- [ ] Internal Slack announcement + FAQ
- [ ] Customer Success talking points
- [ ] Support briefing + FAQ

**Optional/Conditional:**
- [ ] Webinar (if high-value segment or complex feature)
- [ ] Paid social (small budget test, $2K-$5K)
- [ ] Partner newsletter (if partner ecosystem exists)
- [ ] Customer email series (drip campaign vs. one-off)
- [ ] Demo video (screen recording, 60-90 sec)
- [ ] LinkedIn Ads (if enterprise segment)
- [ ] Reddit/community outreach (if product has dev community)

**Timeline:** 8 weeks

**Budget:** $10K-$30K

---

## Tier 3: Minor Launch (Lightweight Campaign)

**Required Channels:**
- [ ] Email (affected segment only, single send)
- [ ] In-product banner (dismissible, low-friction)
- [ ] Blog post (300-500 words, can be bundled with other updates)
- [ ] Changelog (brief entry)
- [ ] Help center update (if workflow changes)
- [ ] Internal Slack announcement (brief)
- [ ] Support FAQ update (if questions expected)

**Optional/Conditional:**
- [ ] Social post (1 organic post, if noteworthy)
- [ ] Sales/CS email update (if customer-facing change)
- [ ] Short demo GIF (embedded in changelog or help doc)

**Timeline:** 3 weeks

**Budget:** $0-$5K

---

## Tier 4: Maintenance (No Campaign)

**Required:**
- [ ] Changelog entry only

**Optional:**
- [ ] Help doc update (if user-facing behavior changes)
- [ ] Internal Slack note (if team needs to know)

**Timeline:** 1 week

**Budget:** $0

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email

**Tier 1:**
- Segment by persona (2-3 variants)
- Full HTML design with hero image
- Multiple CTAs (demo, docs, webinar)
- Send from named executive or PMM
- Follow-up sequence (3-5 emails over 30 days)

**Tier 2:**
- Segment by use case or plan type
- Standard template (branded but not custom design)
- One primary CTA
- Send from PMM or product team
- Single send (no sequence unless high engagement)

**Tier 3:**
- Single segment (users affected by the change)
- Plain text or minimal design
- One CTA
- Send from product team
- No follow-up

---

### Blog Post

**Tier 1:**
- 1000-1500 words
- Thought leadership angle (why now, industry context)
- 3-5 screenshots or visuals
- Customer quote or case study embedded
- SEO-optimized
- Promoted on homepage
- Translated (if multi-language product)

**Tier 2:**
- 600-800 words
- Product announcement angle (what's new, how to use it)
- 2-3 screenshots
- Link to demo or docs
- Basic SEO
- Standard blog promotion

**Tier 3:**
- 300-500 words
- Release notes style (what changed, quick how-to)
- 1-2 screenshots or none
- Link to help docs
- Minimal promotion

---

### In-Product Messaging

**Tier 1:**
- Multi-step modal with product tour
- Tooltips on new UI elements
- Persistent banner (week 1)
- Empty state messaging (if new section)
- Onboarding checklist (if complex)

**Tier 2:**
- Single modal with "Learn More" CTA
- Dismissible banner (3-7 days)
- Optional tooltip on primary action

**Tier 3:**
- Dismissible banner only (1-3 days)
- Or: tooltip on changed element
- Or: nothing (passive discovery)

---

### Sales Enablement

**Tier 1:**
- Live training session (1 hour, recorded)
- Sales deck (15-20 slides)
- Battlecard (2 pages: positioning, objections, talk tracks)
- Demo script with talk tracks
- Pricing/packaging guide
- Competitive positioning doc
- Internal FAQ (20-30 questions)
- Role-play scenarios
- Certification or quiz (optional)

**Tier 2:**
- Async training video (10-15 min, recorded)
- Sales deck (8-10 slides)
- One-pager (positioning, key points, demo flow)
- Talk tracks (3-5 key messages)
- Internal FAQ (10-15 questions)

**Tier 3:**
- Slack announcement with key points
- One-pager (1 page: what changed, how to demo)
- Link to help docs
- Internal FAQ (5 questions)

---

### Social Media

**Tier 1:**
- Campaign: 4-6 weeks of coordinated posts
- LinkedIn: 6-8 posts (company + employee advocacy)
- X/Twitter: 8-12 posts + thread
- Paid amplification ($5K-$20K budget)
- Employee advocacy kit (sample posts for team to share)
- Engage with comments/replies
- Community outreach (Reddit, HN, relevant forums)

**Tier 2:**
- Organic posts: 2-3 over 2 weeks
- LinkedIn: 2-3 posts (company page + PMM personal)
- X/Twitter: 2-4 posts
- Optional paid ($2K-$5K if budget)
- Basic employee advocacy (share with team, optional for them)

**Tier 3:**
- 1 organic post (company LinkedIn or X)
- Or: skip entirely

---

### Webinars

**Tier 1:**
- Live webinar (30-45 min)
- Registration landing page
- Email promotion sequence
- Q&A session
- Recording + clips for social
- Follow-up email with resources

**Tier 2:**
- Optional: live demo (20-30 min)
- Or: pre-recorded walkthrough
- Invite via email (no landing page needed)
- Q&A optional

**Tier 3:**
- Skip (too much overhead)

## Choosing Channels (Decision Framework)

### Start with Tier Requirements

Each tier has **required channels** — these are non-negotiable.

### Add Optional Channels Based On:

**Budget:**
- Low budget → skip paid, webinars, video production
- High budget → add paid, events, PR, analyst relations

**Audience:**
- Enterprise → add webinars, analyst briefings, sales enablement depth
- SMB → focus on self-serve (blog, in-product, help docs)
- Developer → add GitHub, Stack Overflow, technical blog, code examples

**Product Complexity:**
- Complex product → add webinar, demo video, step-by-step guides
- Simple feature → skip; blog + in-product is enough

**Competitive Context:**
- First-to-market → add PR, thought leadership, analyst outreach
- Playing catch-up → focus on customers (email, enablement) over external noise

**Past Performance (Brain Section 7):**
- If past launches show webinars drive 40% of conversions → prioritize
- If paid social consistently underperforms → skip

## Tactical Sequencing (By Week)

### Tier 1 (12-Week Timeline)

**Week -12 to -9: Foundation**
- Positioning workshop
- Competitive analysis
- Customer research / beta feedback synthesis
- Message development

**Week -9 to -6: Content Creation**
- Blog post draft
- Sales deck v1
- Demo script
- Press release draft
- Landing page design

**Week -6 to -4: Asset Production**
- Video production
- Design assets (social, email, web)
- Help docs written
- FAQ developed
- Sales training materials finalized

**Week -4 to -2: Campaign Setup**
- Email sequences built
- Social calendar scheduled
- Paid campaigns configured
- PR outreach begins
- Webinar promoted

**Week -2 to -1: Pre-Launch**
- Internal briefings (sales, CS, support)
- Press embargo (if applicable)
- Customer advisory board preview
- Final QA on all assets

**Week 0: Launch**
- Press release goes live (morning)
- Blog post published (morning)
- Email sent (mid-morning)
- Social posts go live (staggered through day)
- In-product messaging activates
- Sales/CS begin outreach
- Monitor coverage, engagement, bugs

**Week +1 to +4: Post-Launch**
- Webinar (week 1 or 2)
- Follow-up emails
- Paid campaigns optimize
- Monitor metrics
- Customer feedback synthesis
- Bug fixes prioritized

---

### Tier 2 (8-Week Timeline)

**Week -8 to -6: Foundation**
- Positioning (or pull from brain)
- Message development
- Identify target segment

**Week -6 to -4: Content Creation**
- Blog post draft
- Sales deck
- Email copy
- Help docs

**Week -4 to -2: Asset Production**
- Design work (email, social, in-product)
- Sales enablement video
- Social posts written

**Week -2 to -1: Campaign Setup**
- Email built and scheduled
- Social scheduled
- In-product messaging configured
- Sales/CS briefed

- Blog + email + in-product + social go live (coordinated)
- Changelog published
- Sales/CS notified

**Week +1 to +4: Post-Launch**
- Optional webinar (week 1-2)
- Monitor metrics
- Customer feedback collection
- Retro at week 4

---

### Tier 3 (3-Week Timeline)

**Week -3: Planning**
- Define scope and messaging (pull from brain if available)
- Identify affected users

**Week -2: Content Creation**
- Blog post (short)
- Email copy
- Help docs update
- In-product messaging copy

**Week -1: Production**
- Email scheduled
- In-product banner configured
- Changelog drafted
- Support briefed

**Week 0: Launch**
- Everything goes live same day
- Monitor for support tickets

## Self-Learning: Adjusting Tactics Over Time

After each launch, capture channel performance in brain Section 7:

```markdown
**Channel Performance:**
- Webinar: 287 registrants, 42% conversion to trial (🔥 worked)
- Paid social (LinkedIn): 1.8% CTR, $45 CPA (❌ didn't work)
- Email (enterprise segment): 9% open, 2.1% click (⚠️ underperformed)
- In-product modal: 67% click-through (🔥 worked)

**Lesson:**
For future enterprise technical features:
- ✅ Prioritize: Webinars, in-product, technical blog
- ❌ Skip: Paid social (doesn't work for this segment)
- ⚠️ Fix: Email subject lines (test in next launch)
```
