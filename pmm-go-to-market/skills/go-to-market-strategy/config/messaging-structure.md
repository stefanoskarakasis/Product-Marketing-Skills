# Messaging Structure by Tier

## T1 Messaging Structure

**Headline:** Customer outcome (not feature)
Example: "Finally, enterprise-grade security that doesn't slow your team down"

**Supporting pillars:**
1. What changed (feature/capability)
2. Why now (market context)
3. Who benefits (persona)
4. Proof (customer quote, metrics, or case study)

**Channels:**
- Press release → TechCrunch, industry pubs
- Analyst briefing → Gartner, Forrester
- Webinar → Live demo + Q&A
- Email campaign → Multi-touch sequence
- Sales deck → Updated competitive messaging
- Landing page → Full customer journey

**Tone:** Strategic, confident, outcome-focused

---

## T2 Messaging Structure

**Hook:** Problem statement
Example: "How to scale customer success without doubling your team"

**Proof:** 2-3 supporting details
- Feature highlights
- Customer results
- Comparison to alternatives

**CTA:** Clear next step (demo, signup, meeting)

**Channels:**
- Email announcement
- Sales one-pager
- Webinar/lunch & learn
- Slack/community post

**Tone:** Professional, benefit-focused, accessible

---

## T3 Messaging Structure

**Release note format:**
- What: 1-2 sentence feature description
- Why: Customer benefit
- Who: Target user role

**Channels:**
- Release notes (product)
- Email (opt-in)
- In-app notification
- Slack

**Tone:** Direct, informative

---

## T4 Messaging Structure

**Changelog entry:**
- Feature name
- Benefit (1 sentence)

**Channels:**
- Release notes only
- Internal Slack

**Tone:** Matter-of-fact
