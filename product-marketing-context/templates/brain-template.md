## Notes on Variables

**Template variable naming convention:**

```
{{product_name}}              ← Simple field
{{icp_pain_points_1}}         ← Array item 1
{{alternatives_primary_1}}    ← Array item 1
{{voice_attributes}}          ← String list (comma-separated)
{{generated_date}}            ← Current date (auto-filled)
{{brain_owner}}               ← User's name or email
{{last_updated}}              ← Date of last edit
```

When populating the template in the skill:
1. Replace all `{{variable}}` with actual values from wizard answers
2. Remove blank `{{variable}}` lines (don't leave empty lines)
3. If a user skipped a section, note it: "*(Skipped during setup)*"
4. Always include the `{{brain_owner}}` and `{{generated_date}}`

## Example: Populated Brain (Reference)

If a user fills out the wizard, here's what a populated brain looks like:

```markdown
# PMM Brain — HubSpot

**Generated:** 2026-06-11
**Version:** 2.2.0
**Owner:** Sarah Chen (sarah@example.com)

---

## Section 1: Product Context

**Product Name:** 
HubSpot

**Product Description:**
HubSpot is an all-in-one CRM platform that unifies sales, marketing, and customer service. We solve the problem of disconnected tools that create blind spots and slow growth. Instead of 8 different tools, one integrated platform.

**Stage:** 
Scale (market leader, optimizing)

**Target Market:**
Mid-market B2B SaaS companies (50–500 employees) in the sales, marketing, and customer success space. Our sweet spot is companies that just crossed $5M ARR and need to professionalize their GTM motion.

**Value Proposition:**
HubSpot helps growing B2B companies unify their GTM data so they can make faster decisions and grow revenue predictably.

---

## Section 2: ICP Definition

**Company Size:**
50–500 employees OR $5M–$50M ARR

**Industry/Vertical:**
Horizontal — any B2B SaaS company. But our highest-fit segments are: Sales enablement tools, Marketing automation, Customer success platforms.

**Geography:**
North America (US + Canada), with growing adoption in EMEA

**Primary Persona:**
VP of Sales — responsible for pipeline, quota, and team productivity. Reports to CRO or CEO. Owns the sales tech stack. Wants to prove ROI.

**Economic Buyer:**
CRO or CFO for deals over $100k/year. For smaller deals ($20k–$100k), VP of Sales can approve.

**Champion Profile:**
Sales operations manager or revenue analyst. Frustrated with manual reporting. Wants to look good to their VP by saving time and increasing forecast accuracy.

**Top 3 Pain Points:**
1. Disconnected data across Salesforce + email + Slack. Takes 10+ hours/week to manually compile reports.
2. Can't forecast accurately because data is always stale.
3. Can't prove sales productivity (deals won/lost, time to close, conversion rates).

**Buying Triggers:**
Just raised funding and need to professionalize GTM. Got a new CRO who demands better data. Trying to optimize sales process before hiring more reps.

---

## Section 3: Alternatives & Positioning

**Named Alternatives (Top 3):**
1. Salesforce (incumbent, expensive, complex for SMB)
2. Pipedrive (simpler, cheaper, but limited integration)
3. Klaviyo (alternative if customer-focused, not sales-focused)

**Status Quo (If they don't buy anything):**
Manual Salesforce + email + Slack. Sales manager spends 10+ hours/week building custom reports in Excel. Forecasting is reactive, not predictive.

**Why They Leave Alternatives:**
Salesforce is too expensive ($3k+/month for core + add-ons) and requires an admin. Pipedrive is cheaper but doesn't integrate with Slack or email. They leave because status quo is broken but alternatives feel incomplete.

**Unique Capabilities:**
We're the only platform that combines CRM + email + Slack + documents + analytics in one interface. Salesforce you need integrations for. Pipedrive is simpler but less powerful. We're simple AND powerful.

**Category:**
All-in-One CRM for Sales-Led Growth Companies

**Market Type:**
Resegmented market (CRM category exists, but we resegmented it around "sales-led growth companies" instead of "enterprises").

---

## Section 4: Voice & Tone

**Brand Voice Attributes:**
Direct, practical, data-driven, operator-focused, no hype

**Tone Shifts by Persona:**
- **VP of Sales:** Strategic, ROI-focused, outcome-oriented. "Help me prove this works. Show me impact."
- **Sales Ops Manager:** Tactical, specific, practitioner-focused. "How do I set this up in 1 day?"
- **CRO/CEO:** Board-level, business outcome, risk/opportunity. "How does this de-risk our growth?"

**Language Preferences:**
We say "revenue team" not "sales team". We say "GTM motion" not "sales process". We say "forecast accuracy" not "prediction".

**Forbidden Phrases:**
Never say "game-changer", "revolutionary", "one-stop-shop", "synergy". Avoid "AI-powered" unless genuinely a core differentiator (we don't use it). Never compare to Salesforce without data (we lose that comparison).

**Tone Example:**
"Sales ops managers shouldn't spend 10 hours a week in Excel. Salesforce has the data. Email has the interaction history. Slack has the context. HubSpot brings it together so you can build a forecast that actually matches reality. No custom scripts. No late-night Excel sessions. Just numbers that your VP can trust."

---

## Section 5: Market Context

**Market Maturity:**
Growing (multiple CRM players exist, buyers are aware, but consolidation is happening)

**Macro Forces Making Our Solution Relevant:**
1. Remote work explosion → need for async collaboration tools that integrate CRM data
2. Sales tech stack fragmentation → buyers are tired of switching between tools
3. Data as competitive advantage → buyers now expect real-time intelligence built into their CRM
4. AI adoption → predictive forecasting + automation is table stakes

**Why Now:**
Buyers need unified GTM platforms NOW because the old model of disconnected sales, marketing, and CS tools is breaking down. Teams need to move faster, and that's impossible when data is spread across 8 tools.

**Market Narrative (The bigger story):**
The future of GTM is unified, data-driven, and AI-augmented. Companies that stick with legacy point solutions (Salesforce + Marketo + separate analytics) will lose to those with integrated platforms. We're not just a CRM. We're the operating system for modern GTM teams.

---

## Section 6: Proof Points Registry

**Approved Metrics:**
- 89% customer retention rate (from Salesforce, Q4 2025 earnings)
- 10.5k customers (public announcement, Jan 2026)
- $800M ARR (Q4 2025 earnings)
- 4.2 stars on G2 (data pulled weekly)
- 95% uptime SLA (from our status page)

**Customer Quotes & Case Study Results:**
- "HubSpot reduced our sales cycle by 40% in 6 months" — VP Sales, Acme Corp (case study available)
- "We cut our reporting time from 10 hours/week to 2 hours/week" — Sales Ops Manager, TechCorp
- Case study: Acme Corp increased pipeline by 3x in 6 months using HubSpot's forecasting

**Forbidden Claims:**
Don't claim "industry-leading" without data. Don't cite customer names without written approval (get case study signed). Don't promise specific ROI ($100k saved) without customer context (different for every company). Don't compare feature-by-feature to Salesforce without external review (we usually lose that comparison).
```
